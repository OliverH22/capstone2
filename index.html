WHY OCEAN POLLUTION?

Plastic pollution in our oceans is a significant environmental threat to the planet.

By getting a better understanding of pollution, we can help put an end to it.

SIMULATION

QUANTITY

HOW MUCH PLASTIC IS IN THE OCEAN?

Short answer? Lots. Humans have been dumping plastics in the ocean at a rate of about 8 million tons per year.

This plastic comes in different forms, ranging from tiny particles called microplastics, up to larger pieces like bottles and packages.

The amount of plastic in the ocean has grown exponentially since 1950, and will continue to grow unless we act now.

THE SOURCE

WHERE DOES OCEAN PLASTIC COME FROM?

While some plastic pollution comes from individuals throwing garbage out, the majority is actully from big corporations disposing of waste.

Believe it or not, most of the world's plastic pollution comes from just a few regions.

What's importahnd to understand here is that the only way to create real change is to hold corporations accountable. The best way to do that is by supporting ocean-friendly policies and regulations.

EFFECTS

THE DANGERS OF OCEAN PLASTICS

Though large plastics are the obvious threat, microplastics are incredibly harmful too.

Fish and other marine life often eat these tiny plastic particles, which can be harmful and potentially toxic to animals.

Plastic ingested by fish breaks down into potentially toxic fibers, which make their way up the food chain - even to humans.
